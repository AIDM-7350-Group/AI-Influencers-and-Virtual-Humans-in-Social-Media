WHAT IS A VIRTUAL HUMAN?

The concept of virtual human

With the continuous development of information technology, virtual humans have become a reality in recent years and have gradually become more active. Virtual humans typically have four notable characteristics. Firstly, as the name suggests, virtual humans are different from real people in the real world, they exist in the virtual world. Secondly, virtual humans integrate multiple technologies, including computer graphics, motion capture, machine learning, high-precision rendering, and speech synthesis. Thirdly, virtual humans have anthropomorphic features. They usually have a physical appearance, body type, and personality similar to those of real people, and are endowed with the behavioral characteristics of real people, such as language, expressions, and actions. Finally, compared to real people, the image, personality, and background story of virtual humans are all determined by the creators, providing a larger space for shaping. (Cui & Liu, 2023)

The concept of virtual influencer

Another concept that often co-occurs with the concept of virtual humans is virtual influencers. Virtual influencers are also known as CGI influencers or AI influencers. They are virtual humans created by various technologies, active on social media and other online platforms, and controlled by artificial intelligence or designers. Some virtual influencers are designed as animated characters, while others are extremely realistic, possessing highly realistic appearances, personalities, and features, making them almost indistinguishable from real people. Broadly speaking, virtual influencers can be classified into three categories: humanoid virtual influencers (HVI), anime-like virtual influencers (AVI), and non-humanoid virtual influencers (NVI) (Kim & Wang, 2024). These types of virtual influencers attract different audiences. They accumulate fans through content sharing, interaction, and commercial collaborations, and are mainly applied in fields such as brand endorsements, live streaming sales, and fashion marketing.

Some confusing concepts

Often, people tend to confuse virtual humans and VTubers, but they are fundamentally different. Although VTubers are also called virtual hosts, their essence is a virtual image driven by a real person, relying on real people for real-time motion capture, voice acting and performance. The core is real person control. While virtual humans can operate independently by AI, they do not necessarily rely on real-time human control. Virtual humans are quite different from other AI-controlled technologies, such as chatbots and deepfakes. Chatbots focus on text interaction and do not have a complete humanoid appearance or personality, emphasizing functional conversations rather than image creation. Deepfakes are a technology that uses AI to replace or forge the images and voices of existing real people, aiming to imitate a specific real person and pose risks of identity fraud. However, virtual humans do not have such moral risks; they are original digital characters.

Why are they popular on social media?

A virtual influencer is defined as a character created by non-human digital means. They share content on social media and interact with consumers, and have gained a certain level of influence on current social media platforms. There are several reasons why they are popular.

✨

the appearance is stable

For humans, with changes in time or lifestyle, changes in appearance and body shape are inevitable. However, virtual influencers can maintain their image forever, which enables them to perfectly align with the brand image and brand concept, and there will be no situations where they may become overweight or age, thus avoiding any potential incompatibility with the brand image.

🌌

the brand has extremely strong control

As digital entities, virtual influencers do not face the constraints of physicality and time and space like real-life influencers do. Virtual influencers do not get tired, hungry, or sick, and they can be placed in any scenario, even in planets or the universe, for the brand to shoot creative scenes. Therefore, virtual influencers can be everywhere and perform any tasks at any time (Mouritzen et al., 2024). More importantly, their words and deeds in advertisements are completely controlled by the brand. This complete controllability ensures that virtual influencers can always act in accordance with the brand's values and marketing strategies, becoming an excellent marketing tool.

🛡️

no celebrity scandals

Real-life influencers may encounter negative public opinions regarding personal moral issues, which could seriously damage the reputation of the brands they endorse. In contrast, virtual influencers do not have a real personal life or independent thoughts, so they are less likely to engage in improper behavior or cause negative public relations crises. This reduces the operational costs for the brands and significantly boosts the brands' confidence in using virtual humans for promotion.

📱

convenient for cross-platform operation

When human influencers operate across platforms, they need to invest a lot of effort and costs (such as the differences between short videos and long-form content). However, virtual influencers can customize the content for various outputs like short videos, long videos, and live broadcasts, without bearing the logistical burdens or schedule conflicts brought about by physical travel and shooting. This adaptability enables brands to achieve extensive market coverage with minimal effort.

How are they created?

The generation of virtual humans usually involves several core technologies.

🖥️

CGI

Computer-generated imagery (CGI) is the fundamental technology for creating the appearance of virtual humans. Through 3D modeling, texture processing, lighting and rendering techniques, designers can construct realistic or stylized body shapes, facial features, skin textures and clothing styles.

📹

Motion Capture

Motion capture, also known as motion tracking, involves recording and processing the movements of humans or objects. It is widely used in various fields. The collected data can be applied to virtual reality, games, biomechanical research, etc. With the development of motion capture technology, it has now become a mainstream technology in the animation film and video game industries. It can convert the movements of actors into realistic animation effects by capturing their actions (Tang et al., 2024). Therefore, in the production of virtual humans, it is used to collect information such as the body movements, facial micro-expressions, and gestures of human actors, enabling the virtual characters to exhibit realistic speaking styles, postures, and emotional expressions.

🧠

Generative AI and human+AI workflow

As for generative AI, this technology has permeated various fields, including the field of virtual humans. The operation of virtual humans relies on a workflow that combines human teams with AI, integrating multi-source information and cross-system collaboration, in order to achieve real-time interaction. For example, in the case of Louise, Louise is a virtual puppet driven by IBM Watson Assistant and Unity. The core AI system is responsible for aggregating multimodal inputs (including sensory signals such as vision), dynamic memory, and external data sources (such as calendars, weather applications). Through pre-trained dialogue skills (intention recognition, dialogue tree management, etc.), the AI system can handle user requests and generate customized response content, which is then transmitted to the Unity engine to drive visual, audio, and physical simulation outputs (Schmidt et al., 2020). During this process, AI is responsible for the organization and manipulation of a large amount of data, while the human team is responsible for training and supervision.
