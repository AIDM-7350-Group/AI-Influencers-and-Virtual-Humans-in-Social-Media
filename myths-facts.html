ZAP! MYTH #1

Virtual influencers are just "animated cartoons".

POW! FACT #1

They are complete "brand media entities". Unlike 2D cartoons, they use real-time rendering and AI to interact with fans. This represents a major shift in the marketing paradigm .

ZAP! MYTH #2

They only matter in the fashion industry.

POW! FACT #2

Their scope is much broader—from retail (Lu do Magalu) to healthcare and education. They are being deployed as 24/7 "digital employees".

ZAP! MYTH #3

Audiences never trust them because they are "fake".

Click to reveal the truth →

POW! FACT #3

Trust is driven by source credibility and perceived authenticity, not biological existence. Their "transparent fiction" and consistent values often make them more trusted than human celebrities.

ZAP! MYTH #4

Brands use them only because they are cheaper.

Click to reveal the truth →

POW! FACT #4

Cost isn't the primary driver; "controllability" and "brand safety" are. High-fidelity avatars require significant R&D, but they don't get involved in scandals.

ZAP! MYTH #5

Regulation is unnecessary because everyone knows they're virtual.

POW! FACT #5

As lines blur, governance is essential to prevent deepfakes. Countries like China are already drafting rules for mandatory labeling of digital humans.

THE BOTTOM LINE

Virtual influencers are neither magical solutions nor existential threats. They represent a new creative medium that requires thoughtful implementation, clear disclosure, and ethical consideration.
